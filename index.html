What is Oral Medicine in Canada?

In Canada, Oral Medicine functions under a broader, more integrated medical framework than its United States counterparts. It bridges dentistry, pathology, and systemic internal medicine to provide full regional diagnostic and therapeutic intervention.

"The Canadian scope explicitly defines the specialty as encompassing advanced therapeutics for complex mucosal anomalies, systemic pathodynamic manifestations, regional neuro-vascular disorders, and full cross-disciplinary diagnostic medicine."

01

Orofacial Pain Management

Comprehensive tracking and treatment of chronic non-odontogenic pain matrices, neurovascular disorders, and advanced temporomandibular structural complications.

02

Dental Sleep Medicine

Multidisciplinary design and monitoring protocols for oral appliance therapies managing obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) and systemic sleep fragmentation patterns.

03

Mucosal Pathology

Histological analysis, diagnostic monitoring, and immunomodulatory therapeutic programs for chronic, infectious, auto-immune, and pre-malignant oral mucosal conditions.

04

Salivary & Systemic Care

Clinical management of salivary gland hyper/hypofunction, xerostomia pathodynamics, and oral manifestations of complex systemic chemotherapy or radiotherapy regimens.
